What data may be processed

Purposes, legal basis and retention

Handling enquiries and bookings

Contact details, stay requests and booking preferences. Legal basis: pre-contractual measures and contract. Retention: up to 12 months for unsuccessful enquiries, and for as long as needed to manage confirmed stays.

Check-in, administrative and tax duties

Guest identity data, documents required by law, invoicing data and city-tax related information. Legal basis: legal obligation. Retention: for the periods required by hospitality, accounting and administrative rules.

Website security and abuse prevention

Technical logs and access information needed to protect the site and investigate anomalies. Legal basis: legitimate interest in securing the service. Retention: only for the time strictly necessary to analyse and resolve the issue.

Optional analytics

Data collected by measurement tools enabled only after consent. Legal basis: consent. Retention: according to the configured tool and until consent is withdrawn or the site setup changes.

Recipients and third-party services

Personal data may be processed by providers supporting the website and the stay, only where needed to deliver the requested service. This site integrates or links to:

  • Booking engine on book.bnbdimoraorru.it for availability, reservations and part of the pre-arrival flow.
  • WhatsApp when you choose to contact the property through that channel.
  • Google Maps for map embeds displayed directly on site pages.
  • Google Tag Manager / analytics tools configured in the container, only after analytics consent.
  • External review or travel platforms only when you decide to open outgoing links to those services.

Whether data provision is mandatory

Providing the data required for enquiries, bookings, check-in and legal obligations is optional but necessary to arrange and manage the stay. Providing data for analytics or external media is optional and depends entirely on your consent choices.

International transfers

Some technology providers may process data outside the European Economic Area. In such cases the processing relies on the contractual and organisational safeguards made available by the provider and required by applicable law.

Your rights
